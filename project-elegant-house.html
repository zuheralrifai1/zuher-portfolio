Brand Strategy & Identity Development

About The Brand

The Elegant House is a new upholstery and furniture design brand specialising in custom-made, high-quality pieces. The business needed a complete strategic foundation — from brand identity to marketing materials and online presence — to establish credibility, attract customers, and differentiate itself in a competitive local market.

My Role

  • Discovery & research
  • Brand strategy (positioning, messaging, tone of voice)
  • Full visual identity development
  • Brand guidelines creation
  • Website design & launch
  • Marketing materials & operational assets
  • Brand rollout support

Outcome

A clear understanding of the brand’s purpose, ambition, and strategic needs, resulting in a focused strategic direction that guides the brand’s identity, tone, and visual style.

Target Audience Analysis

The market values craftsmanship, customisation, and high-quality finishes. Customers want pieces that elevate their homes and reflect personal style.

Homeowners Seeking Personalised Style

Profile Age 30–55, stable income, suburban/semi-rural. Aesthetic-driven.
Motivations Custom pieces reflecting taste, design upgrades.
Pain Points Limited customisation, hard to find trustworthy local upholsterers.

Interior Enthusiasts & DIY Upgraders

Profile Age 25–45, first-time homeowners. Trend-focused, confident mixing styles.
Motivations Affordable upgrades, experimenting with style.
Pain Points Poor-quality finishes, confusing fabric choices.

Premium Craftsmanship Seekers

Profile Age 40–65, higher income. Appreciate heritage and tradition.
Motivations Long-term investment pieces, upgrade sentimental furniture.
Pain Points Lack of brands balancing modern design with craftsmanship.

Positioning Statement

Brand Story

The Elegant House was created from a simple belief: homes deserve design that feels personal, intentional, and rooted in real craftsmanship. From the beginning, the brand aimed to move away from generic décor and instead build pieces that carry meaning — through texture, material, and careful attention to detail. The identity reflects this philosophy: calm, refined, and grounded in a modern interpretation of heritage. Every visual and verbal element was shaped to express trust, care, and an uncompromising commitment to quality, allowing the brand to feel both contemporary and enduring.

Purpose

To create a brand that feels crafted, trustworthy, and rooted in timeless design principles.

Mission

To help customers understand the value of skilled craftsmanship through clear communication and consistent visual expression.

Values

We believe in simplicity, honesty, and attention to detail — qualities that guide every decision in the brand’s identity.

Tagline Concepts

  • Crafted With Purpose.
  • Built to Last.
  • Where Design Meets Craft.

Tone of Voice

A clear, confident, and human way of speaking.

Do

  • • Use simple, direct language that is easy for anyone to understand.
  • • Speak with confidence and clarity, without sounding aggressive.
  • • Focus on the customer’s needs, not internal jargon.
  • • Explain ideas in a structured, calm, and helpful way.
  • • Use warm, human phrasing that feels approachable.
  • • Give direction through clear statements, not decorative language.
  • • Use storytelling when it helps someone understand the meaning behind the brand.

Don't

  • • Don't use overly complex or academic sentences.
  • • Don't speak in a salesy or exaggerated tone.
  • • Don't use metaphors or emotional language that feels disconnected from the brand.
  • • Don't overload sentences with adjectives.
  • • Don't shift between formal and casual tone inconsistently.
  • • Don't use humour or slang unless it supports the brand’s identity.
  • • Don't rely on icons, labels, or colours to communicate tone — let the text do the work.

Example Phrases

“We believe in clarity — every step should feel simple and purposeful.”

“Our role is to guide, not overwhelm.”

“Here’s what this means for you…”

“Let’s make this easier to understand.”

07

Logo System

Primary Logo

The primary wordmark is the main identifier for The Elegant House.
It represents the brand’s full expression — refined, calm, and rooted in craftsmanship.

Stacked Logo

This alternate layout offers a more compact structure while maintaining elegance and legibility.
It is ideal when space is limited but the brand still needs a premium presence.

Initial Mark “E” (Monogram)

The monogram is a simplified brand asset used when the full wordmark would be too detailed or overpowering.
It preserves brand recognition in small spaces.

Important Visual Rules

  • Background must be white, not cream.
  • Logos must be in Hague Blue (#3D4E57).
  • Spacing inside the logo panel: 48–72px padding.
  • No harsh shadows — keep it high-end editorial.
